# MetLife Stadium — Emergency, Medical & Crowd Protocols (FIFA World Cup 2026)

## Medical Emergency Response

### First Aid Stations
There are 4 primary First Aid Stations located throughout MetLife Stadium:

1. **Medical Zone 1 — Gate A / North Concourse**
   - Location: Level 1, near Section 101
   - Staffed by: 2 EMTs, 1 Registered Nurse
   - Average response time: 3 minutes

2. **Medical Zone 2 — Gate D / West Concourse**
   - Location: Level 1, near Section 144
   - Staffed by: 2 EMTs, 1 Registered Nurse
   - Average response time: 3 minutes

3. **Medical Zone 3 — Level 2 South**
   - Location: Plaza Level, near Section 224
   - Staffed by: 3 EMTs, 1 Physician Assistant
   - Average response time: 2.5 minutes

4. **Medical Zone 4 — Plaza Level (Primary)**
   - Location: Plaza Level, near Section 109 (main station)
   - Staffed by: 4 EMTs, 2 Registered Nurses, 1 ER Physician on-call
   - Equipment: AED, oxygen, trauma kits, wheelchair stretchers
   - Average response time: 2 minutes
   - **This is the primary medical command center for large-scale incidents.**

5. **Medical Zone 5 — Upper Level**
   - Location: Level 3, near Section 315
   - Staffed by: 1 EMT, 1 Nurse
   - Average response time: 4 minutes

## Crowd Control & Surge Protocols

### Gate Capacity Thresholds
Each gate has a defined maximum throughput to prevent crowd surge:
- **Gate A (North)**: Max 12,000 guests/hour. Surge Protocol activates at 10,500/hour.
- **Gate B (East)**: Max 14,000 guests/hour. Surge Protocol activates at 11,000/hour. *Highest traffic gate.*
- **Gate C (South)**: Max 13,000 guests/hour. Surge Protocol activates at 10,500/hour.
- **Gate D (West)**: Max 10,000 guests/hour. Surge Protocol activates at 8,500/hour.

### Surge Protocol Steps (Activated by Crowd Control Command Center)
1. **Level 1 Alert (Yellow)**: Deploy 4 additional lane officers. Open overflow screening lanes. Notify transit authority.
2. **Level 2 Alert (Orange)**: Halt new entries for 5-minute intervals. Route guests to alternate gates via PA announcement. Deploy motorcycle units to parking areas.
3. **Level 3 Alert (Red)**: Full gate pause. Contact NJ State Police for crowd management support. Activate emergency lighting in overflow areas. Medical teams on standby outside perimeter.

### Crowd Density Monitoring
- Cameras and occupancy sensors monitor crowd density in real-time across 12 zones.
- Zone density thresholds:
  * Green: < 2 persons/m² — Normal operations
  * Yellow: 2–3 persons/m² — Monitoring required
  * Orange: 3–4 persons/m² — Intervention required
  * Red: > 4 persons/m² — Immediate action required (evacuation possible)

## Evacuation Procedures

### General Evacuation
- Evacuation will be announced via the PA system and flashing emergency lights.
- All guests must proceed calmly to the nearest exit gate.
- **Do NOT use elevators during an evacuation.**
- Use ramps and stairwells only.
- Staff in orange vests are assigned as evacuation wardens for each section.

### Priority Evacuation Routes
- Sections 100–115: Exit via Gate A (North)
- Sections 116–128: Exit via Gate B (East)
- Sections 129–143: Exit via Gate C (South)
- Sections 144–150: Exit via Gate D (West)
- Upper Level guests use the main ramps at North and South corners first.

### Shelter-in-Place Protocol
- In the event of a severe weather warning (tornado, lightning strike within 8 miles), a shelter-in-place order is issued.
- All guests must move to interior concourse areas (away from open seating).
- Estimated shelter duration: until weather clears, announced via PA.

### Bomb Threat / Active Threat Protocol
- Controlled evacuation by section, directed by security staff.
- Guests should leave all belongings behind and follow security directions.
- Assembly points are located in parking Lots E, F, G (North and West) and Lots K, L (South).
- Do not re-enter the stadium unless cleared by law enforcement.

## Weather & Environmental Protocols

### Rain & Lightning
- Ponchos available for purchase at concession stands (Level 1 and Level 3) — $5 each.
- If lightning is detected within 8 miles, the match may be suspended (FIFA protocol).
- Suspension announcement made via PA. Guests proceed to covered concourse areas.
- Match resumes once lightning is cleared for 30 consecutive minutes.

### Extreme Heat (Above 95°F)
- Cooling stations activated at Level 1 (near Gate A and Gate C) and Level 2 (Section 224).
- Free water distributed at all hydration zones.
- Medical staff on elevated alert for heat exhaustion cases.

### Extreme Cold (Below 32°F)
- Warming stations with hot beverages activated near Gate A (Level 1) and Gate D (Level 1).
- Extra blankets available at Guest Services (Gate A, Level 1).
